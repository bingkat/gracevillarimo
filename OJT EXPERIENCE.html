OJT EXPERIENCE

I planned this particular internship to gain practical experience and exposure to the IT industry. I wanted to apply the knowledge and skills that I learned in the classroom to real-world situations and challenges. I also wanted to learn from experienced professionals and gain insights into the latest trends and developments in the industry.

I was given the responsibility of being the lead profiler, which is quite difficult because we have an eight-hour deadline to complete 1,200 quota. Despite the fact that we cannot avoid becoming bored at work, we yet love it due of our coworkers, who occasionally lend a hand when necessary.

The BPO firm I used to work as intern for specialized in making outbound and incoming calls for businesses of all sizes. The organization had a group of highly skilled call center agents, a team leader, HR officers, and other staff members to offer customers high-quality solutions.

I am aware of how crucial on-the-job training is to my course. On-the-job training, commonly referred to as an internship or work placement, is essential for preparing students for the demands and challenges of the IT sector in the real world. In the realm of information technology, on-the-job training is crucial. It bridges the gap between academic knowledge and practical application while providing me with useful skills, industry exposure, and networking possibilities. It is crucial in assisting me in getting ready for a fruitful profession in the quickly developing IT sector.

A good IT professional has a strong foundation in technical knowledge and skills related to their area of expertise. They stay up-to-date with the latest technologies, trends, and industry best practices to effectively address and resolve complex IT issues.
